I started working at my current job a little over a year ago, it was a big step up from where I was at. I was replacing one out
of the two developers at the company, the company's Lead/DevOps/FullStack dev. He also helped with various other IT related tasks. I was nervous to take the role but excited about all the learning opportunities. I had only worked as an application developer and the only web server experience I had was tinkering with my Raspberry Pi at home. 

Being that is was relatively small company, word got around quickly that I was the new "web guy" and all web related issues/questions should be forwarded to me, anything else, to the only IT guy who'd worked there 20+ years. But as anyone working in tech has probably experienced, people who know you have knowledge of computers will pick your brain for anything technology related. It's something I've experienced time and again ever since I got into computers, my family, friends, acquaintances all approaching me for help for things I had no experience with. But it's why I love working in the tech field, I love tinkering, learning new things, and making someone's day better.

So I have a gentlemen introduced to me through our IT guy, he's flustered and a bit anxious to get help with some files that are in the wrong format. He tells me that they just upgraded their system and some software he uses and accidentally converted some files from PDF to PS. He's not sure how it happened but for him to do his job, he needs them changed back. I told him I was not very familiar with working with PDF file nor had I ever heard of PS files. But I had helped people in the past who had accidentally changed file formats and couldn't get their programs to work. So I told him I'd do a little research and get back to him.

I do some Googling and find a free command line program that can convert PS files to PDF. I think to myself, great, I'll just email myself the files, manually change them back and send them back to him. I naively though he was working with 100 or so files and could get it done in a couple of hours. I find him by his work station and ask him to show me the files...holy nested folders batman! There was some deep deep nesting of those folders and emailing myself those files wasn't gonna work, there was over 9GBs of files that needed to be convereted back. He seemed kinda of embarassed and was unsure exactly how it happened, it seemed like he was using a printing program and the root folder for those files was some sort of 'hot folder' that the program detected changes on and performed operations. 

He took notice of my shook and kept saying if I can't help him it's not a big deal, he could just change the files one at a time when he needed to. But, I have that drive in my to help when I can and use my passion and skills as a programmer to do so. I told him no problem, I found a program that can convert the files and all I need is to write a script to run it. So I zipped up the files, wrote a Python script when I got home and let it run over night. Voila all his files converted back! It felt great to have helped him out and just further hammers home my feeling so blessed to have a career doing something I love.
